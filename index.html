My Journey

I grew up in a very small village near Shirdi, Maharashtra. From childhood, I had a single dream: to become an engineer. After my 12th grade, I was determined to go to an IIT and took a gap year to prepare. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic and a medical emergency in my family, things did not go as planned, and I failed to make it.

I got admission to PES Modern College of Engineering, Pune, in the newly introduced Artificial Intelligence & Data Science (AI & DS) branch. Unfortunately, I had no guidance or mentorship. For the first 2.5 years of college, I fell into the common dilemma: "There are no AI/ML jobs for freshers unless you are from an IIT or a top-tier college." Because of this myth, I spent my early years preparing for DSA and Web Development, completely side-tracking my true interests.

In my 6th semester, I decided to take a calculated risk. I abandoned the dilemma and dedicated 100% of my time, energy, and focus to AI/ML and Generative AI.

The risk paid off. At the start of my 4th year, I secured an onsite AI internship at a Pune-based startup where I worked for 8 months. That opened the floodgates. By the time I graduated, I had secured 5 full-time offers (Valuedx, Harbinger, LTIMindtree, Gravitones, and Big Air Labs) and 10+ total offers/internships. After graduation, I chose Harbinger Group as my launchpad, and subsequently switched to Actalent Services (deputed to Ford Motor Company) as an AI/ML Engineer after 11 months.

Interactive AI/ML Roadmap

This is the exact learning path I recommend to transition from zero to an industry-ready AI/ML Engineer. Click on each card to expand core topics and recommended study resources.

01

Mathematical & Programming Foundations

Python, Numpy, Pandas, Linear Algebra, Probability, Calculus

Before writing machine learning code, you must build robust intuition on how models process data behind the scenes. This stage is crucial for debugging model behavior later on.

Core Concepts to Master:
  • Linear Algebra: Vectors, Matrices, Matrix Multiplication, Eigenvalues/Eigenvectors.
  • Calculus: Derivatives, Chain Rule, Gradient Descent optimization.
  • Probability & Stats: Mean, Variance, Distributions, Bayes Theorem, Hypothesis Testing.
  • Programming: Advanced Python structures, Pandas for EDA, Numpy for vectorization.

Recommended Resources

  • Mathematics for ML (Imperial College)
  • Essence of Linear Algebra (3Blue1Brown)
  • 10 Minutes to Pandas Guide
02

Classical Machine Learning

Scikit-Learn, Regression, Decision Trees, Ensembles, Feature Engineering

Over 80% of structured industry data is still processed using classical ML. Learn algorithms that yield highly explainable results and fast execution.

Core Concepts to Master:
  • Supervised Learning: Linear/Logistic Regression, SVMs, Decision Trees, KNNs.
  • Ensemble Models: Random Forests, Gradient Boosted Trees (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost).
  • Unsupervised Learning: K-Means, PCA, Hierarchical Clustering.
  • Data Pipelines: Scaling, handling missing values, cross-validation, hyperparameter tuning.

Recommended Resources

  • Machine Learning Specialization (Andrew Ng)
  • Official Scikit-Learn Tutorials
  • Kaggle Learn - Intro to Machine Learning
03

Deep Learning & Computer Vision

PyTorch, TensorFlow, CNNs, RNNs, Transfer Learning, OpenCV

Deep Learning scales machine learning to handle unstructured data like images, audio, and large-scale textual sequences. PyTorch is the industry-standard library for implementation.

Core Concepts to Master:
  • Neural Network Basics: Multi-Layer Perceptrons, Activation functions, Backpropagation.
  • Computer Vision: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Object Detection (YOLO), Segmentation.
  • Sequence Modeling: Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), LSTMs, GRUs.
  • Optimization: Learning rates, Dropouts, Batch Normalization, Adam Optimizer.

Recommended Resources

  • Deep Learning Specialization (Andrew Ng)
  • Official PyTorch Tutorials & Recipes
  • Practical Deep Learning for Coders (Fast.ai)
04

Natural Language Processing & LLMs

Transformers, Hugging Face, Attention Mechanism, BERT, GPT Architectures

Understand the transition from classical text embeddings (Word2Vec) to self-attention mechanisms that power modern Large Language Models (LLMs).

Core Concepts to Master:
  • Attention Mechanism: Self-Attention, Multi-Head Attention, Positional Encodings.
  • Transformer Architectures: Encoder-only (BERT), Decoder-only (GPT), Encoder-Decoder (T5).
  • Hugging Face Ecosystem: Tokenizers, Datasets, Pre-trained Models, Pipelines.
  • Model Alignment: Fine-Tuning, Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (LoRA, QLoRA).

Recommended Resources

  • Hugging Face Official NLP Course
  • CS224n: Natural Language Processing (Stanford)
  • Let's build GPT from scratch (Andrej Karpathy)
05

Advanced Generative AI & Agentic Workflows

RAG, LangChain, LlamaIndex, Vector Databases, LangGraph, AI Agents, MCP Servers

This is where the industry is heading. Build systems where LLMs do not just output text, but dynamically call APIs, interact with databases, and make complex sequence decisions autonomously.

Core Concepts to Master:
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG): Naive RAG, Advanced RAG (Query translation, indexing, reranking).
  • Vector Databases: ChromaDB, Pinecone, Qdrant, Milvus.
  • Agentic Frameworks: LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, Model Context Protocol (MCP).
  • LLM Application Lifecycle: Evaluation (Ragas, TruLens), Observability (LangSmith, Phoenix), Cost/Latency optimization.

Recommended Resources

  • DeepLearning.AI Short Courses (GenAI/Agents)
  • LangChain & LangGraph Academy Tutorials
  • LlamaIndex Concepts & Architecture Guide

Frequently Asked Questions

Do freshers really get AI/ML engineering roles directly? +

Yes, absolutely! The idea that freshers cannot get AI/ML roles is a myth. However, companies will not hire freshers just for "knowing the definitions" or doing standard tutorials (like the Titanic dataset). They hire freshers who show robust engineering discipline—knowing how to write clean, modular code, how to build and evaluate RAG systems, deploy APIs, and handle model lifecycle pipelines. Proof of work is everything.

Is an IIT or top-tier college degree mandatory to get shortlisted? +

No. While top-tier college names can help get the first foot in the door, in the current market, startups and modern product companies prioritize technical capabilities. If you have unique projects, co-authored research papers (like DocEase), or open-source contributions, your resume will get shortlisted regardless of your college brand.

How did you manage full-time work and college to score a 9.60 SGPA? +

It requires strict time blocking and prioritizing practical applications over rote memorization. Since my coursework was in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, the engineering concepts I used daily at my internships naturally made the college subjects easier to digest. Applying what you learn in real-world environments is the best way to ace academic tests without spending weeks studying.

How should I prepare for a mock interview or resume review call? +

For a Resume Review, please share your resume (preferably as a PDF/editable link) and links to your top 2 GitHub repositories. For a Mock Interview, let me know the specific role type (e.g., Generalist ML, Generative AI/LLM, Data Analyst) and target companies so I can tailor the questions to mimic their actual screening processes.
